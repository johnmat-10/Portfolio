Phytochemical-Assisted Dye Degradation
Undergrad Dissertation
Title

Extraction of Phytochemicals from Sapindus (Soapnut) and Their Influence on Methylene Blue Dye Degradation

Institute

Cochin University of Science & Technology, Kerala

Aim

To optimize the extraction of bioactive phytochemicals from Sapindus and evaluate their effectiveness in methylene blue dye decolorization in synthetic wastewater, along with their inhibitory effects on microbes in anaerobic sludge.

Outcome

Phytochemical analysis confirmed the presence of saponins, tannins, and terpenoids in Sapindus extracts. These compounds exhibited antimicrobial activity and enabled effective degradation and decolorization of methylene blue dye in synthetic wastewater. The findings support the potential of plant-derived phytochemicals as sustainable and eco-friendly agents for wastewater treatment and environmental remediation.
